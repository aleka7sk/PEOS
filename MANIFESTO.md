# PEOS Manifesto

> Software engineering is not the act of writing code.
> It is the discipline of making sound decisions under uncertainty.

## Why PEOS Exists

Modern AI has dramatically reduced the cost of producing code.

It has **not** reduced the cost of making the right engineering decisions.

Teams still struggle with unclear requirements, inconsistent architecture, undocumented trade-offs, poor traceability, and implementation that drifts away from product goals.

PEOS exists because better software is created by improving the decision-making process—not by generating more code.

Its purpose is to provide an open specification for AI-assisted Product Engineering.

---

## What We Believe

We believe that software development should be driven by explicit reasoning rather than implicit assumptions.

We believe that every implementation should exist because a product need justifies it.

We believe that engineering decisions should be transparent, reviewable, and reproducible.

We believe that AI should strengthen engineering discipline instead of replacing it.

We believe that specifications are more valuable than prompts.

We believe that products should evolve through validated decisions rather than intuition alone.

---

## Our Principles

### Product Before Implementation

Implementation is never the starting point.

Every line of code should trace back to a verified product objective.

### Decisions Before Code

Engineering is a sequence of decisions.

Code is only one possible outcome.

### Explicit Over Implicit

Hidden assumptions become future defects.

Important decisions should always be documented.

### Evidence Over Confidence

Confidence is not proof.

Claims should be supported by requirements, experiments, data, or documented reasoning.

### Simplicity Over Cleverness

Complexity is a cost.

The simplest solution that satisfies the product should be preferred.

### Traceability Over Convenience

Every important artifact should explain:

* why it exists;
* where it came from;
* what decision created it.

### Evolution Over Perfection

Specifications are expected to improve.

Continuous refinement is a feature, not a failure.

---

## What PEOS Is Not

PEOS is not a prompt library.

PEOS is not an AI coding framework.

PEOS is not a project management methodology.

PEOS is not a replacement for software engineers.

PEOS is not tied to a single AI model or vendor.

PEOS is not a collection of best practices without structure.

---

## Our Goal

Our goal is to establish a common engineering language that allows humans and AI systems to collaborate through standardized artifacts, explicit decisions, and verifiable workflows.

The specification should remain stable even as AI systems evolve.

Implementations may change.

The engineering principles should not.

---

## Vision

We envision a future where software projects are no longer defined by prompts, tools, or models.

Instead, they are defined by a shared engineering specification that any capable AI or human team can understand, execute, and validate.

PEOS aims to become that specification.

---

## One Commitment

If a future feature makes PEOS easier to use but weaker as an engineering specification,

**the specification comes first.**
